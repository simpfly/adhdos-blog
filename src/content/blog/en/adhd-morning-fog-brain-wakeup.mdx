For many with ADHD, waking up and getting out of bed is the hardest level of the day.

This is not laziness or a personal failure.

In neurobiology, ADHD brains have lower morning arousal levels.

While neurotypicals experience rapid cortical activation upon waking, the ADHD prefrontal cortex behaves like an old diesel engine, requiring substantial pre-heating.

Forcing yourself to start work immediately triggers anxiety and energy depletion.

We must practice **rhythm tracking** and **energy-flow adaptation** to warm up somatic systems.

---

## Somatic Guidelines to Warm Up Your Brain

Don't expect an instant alert state. Use low-friction warm-ups to spin the gears:

### 1. Build Your Energy Menu Morning Ramps
Keep a card list near your bed, or open the [Energy Menu](https://adhdos.app/energy-menu) in ADHDOS.
Avoid any tasks requiring planning or logical thinking (like looking at schedules or replying to mail). Only place low-friction ramp card options:
*   **Serotonin Ramp**: Open the curtains and stand in the light for 1 minute.
*   **Dopamine Ramp**: Go wash your face with warm water and grab a drink.
*   **Endorphin Ramp**: Stretch your limbs gently while lying down.
Align with your morning low battery. Pick a warm-up action card first to let your body lead your brain.

### 2. Enter Neutral with the Breath Orb
If you feel morning palpitations or brain fog, don't rush into decisions.
Lie flat on your back and open the [Breath Orb](https://adhdos.app/breath-orb) in ADHDOS.
Sync your breathing with its expansion for 2 minutes.
This increases oxygen flow and balances your autonomic nervous system, letting your prefrontal cortex shift gears smoothly.

### 3. Use Light Signals to Boot Up Your Cortex
Light striking your retinas is the physical switch that triggers wakefulness.
Your very first action must be exposing your eyes to bright light (natural or artificial) to suppress melatonin and signal your brainstem to boot up.
