For many autistic (ASD) adults, office communication is not natural intuition—it is a high-energy algorithmic simulation.

In neuro-divergent research, this is called the "Double Empathy Problem": ASD individuals and neurotypicals (NT) have fundamentally different communication styles.

NTs default to implicit, politeness-oriented communication; ASD brains natural default is explicit, fact-driven structured communication.

Trying to guess "what they actually meant" is like running heavy decryption software on a low-spec CPU. It burns your prefrontal energy in minutes, leading to social burnout.

The secret to painless workplace communication is: **do not try to act like a social expert. Outsource your templates.**

## The Autistic Guide to Painless Workplace Communication

Minimize cognitive drag by outsourcing your phrasing:

### 1. The 3-Step Status Update Template
When asked for progress, don't write a narrative. Send a bulleted, fact-based list:
*   **[Completed]**: List 1-2 objective deliverables (e.g., Finished draft of proposal).
*   **[In Progress]**: List current tasks and target delivery dates (e.g., Verifying data sheet, expecting completion Thursday afternoon).
*   **[Blocked/Need Help]**: State what you need from others, or write "None" (e.g., Need marketing metrics from team B).
This plain-text, fact-based structure actually reads as "highly professional" to NT managers.

### 2. The Board Library: Copy-Paste Communication Shields
For requesting leave, asking for updates, or declining scope creep, don't compose drafts from scratch.
Keep standard templates pinned to your ADHDOS [Board](https://adhdos.app/board):
*   **Follow-Up**: *"Hi [Name], I am working on project A. Could you let me know when approval on project B is expected? This timeline dictates when we launch phase two. Thanks!"*
*   **Time Off**: *"Hi [Manager], I plan to take Friday off. I will review urgent messages on Slack once at 5 PM that day. Thanks for approving!"*
When you need to communicate, **copy-paste** from your Board. This physical template repository unloads the worry of finding the right words.
