It is 4:00 PM, and you are frozen in your chair.

The half-finished document on your screen is blinking, and your brain feels like absolute mush. You know you have deadlines looming, but physically, you cannot bring yourself to start.

To "take a quick break," you grab your phone and open social media.

An hour vanishes. You do not feel recharged. Instead, due to light overstimulation and chaotic information fragments, your head hurts even more. Guild and shame creep in: "Why am I wasting my time again?"

This is the classic trap for ADHD brains during low-dopamine states.

We are not lazy. Our brain's dopamine tank is simply empty. Forcing yourself to "just do it" with raw willpower is like flooring the gas pedal on a car with no fuel—it does nothing but burn out the engine.

What we need is gentle, low-friction **dopamine alignment**.

---

## What is a Dopamine Menu (Dopamenu)?

A Dopamine Menu is a curated list of activities that bring you genuine joy and restore your mental energy. 

When your brain crashes and begins hunting for stimulation, you do not have to waste executive function deciding what to do. You simply open your menu and pick a pre-selected option.

The core goal is to shift from unconscious, low-quality dopamine foraging (like endless scrolling) to conscious, high-quality energy replenishment.

By aligning with our neurobiology, we practice smart **energy-flow adaptation** instead of battling our fatigue.

---

## How to Structure Your Dopamine Menu

An effective Dopamine Menu has five distinct courses:

### 1. Appetizers: 5-10 Minute Quick Stims
*   **Traits**: Extremely low startup barrier; quickly wakes up your neural pathways.
*   **Examples**: Dancing to one favorite song, using a massage gun, drinking a large glass of ice water.

### 2. Entrees: 20-30 Minute Deep Recharge
*   **Traits**: Requires a small amount of focus; provides sustainable, clean dopamine.
*   **Examples**: A mindful walk outside, reading a chapter of a book, building Lego, doing a quick craft.

### 3. Desserts: High-Stimulation Treats (with boundaries)
*   **Traits**: Highly addictive; easy to overindulge; needs strict limits.
*   **Examples**: Playing one round of an action game, watching a specific YouTube channel (use a timer).

### 4. Sides: Background Stimulation
*   **Traits**: Low-effort stimuli you can run while completing mundane tasks.
*   **Examples**: Listening to podcasts while washing dishes, playing lo-fi ambient tracks while writing.

### 5. Specials: Weekend Delicacies
*   **Traits**: Time-consuming activities that offer a massive sensory reset.
*   **Examples**: A day of hiking in the forest, attending a live concert, organizing a camping trip.
