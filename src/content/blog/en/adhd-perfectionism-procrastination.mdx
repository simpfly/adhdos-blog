For many with ADHD, we aren't lazy; we are held hostage by perfectionism.

Our brain operates on an "All-or-Nothing" engine: either we achieve 100 points, or we refuse to play.

When facing an important task, our prefrontal cortex builds a grand blueprint. But as we prepare to start, we realize our draft won't live up to the fantasy. This gap triggers a panic response: *You will fail. Stop now to avoid pain.*

Thus, we retreat into our phones.

We need proactive **cognitive unloading** to lower the threshold of entry.

---

## 3 Steps to Shatter Perfectionist Deadlocks

When you are stuck in a fear of starting, use these tools to land:

### 1. Brain Dump: Empty the Threat Register
When you procrastinate, your working memory is occupied by background worries: *What if this is terrible? What if they think I am stupid?*
Use the [Brain Dump](https://adhdos.app/brain-dump) in ADHDOS. Spend 2 minutes writing down every worry and loose thought without editing.
Dumping them onto the screen clears your active RAM and lowers anxiety.

### 2. Reframing: Break the "Must Succeed" Rule
Put your absolute assumptions into the ADHDOS [Cognitive Reframer](https://adhdos.app/reframer):
*   **Perfectionist Brain**: "This draft must be flawless, or it proves I am a failure."
*   **Reframed Reality**: "The purpose of a first draft is to exist, not to be good. I am allowed to write garbage."
*   **Perfectionist Brain**: "I must finish all 3,000 words today, or I failed."
*   **Reframed Reality**: "Writing 50 words today is a win. I can stop anytime."

### 3. Embrace "Good-Enoughism"
The rule of good-enoughism is simple: **"A completed draft of garbage is better than a non-existent masterpiece."**
Give yourself permission to write a mediocre, messy draft. When you accept "good enough," your brain's alarm turns off, giving you the freedom to move.
