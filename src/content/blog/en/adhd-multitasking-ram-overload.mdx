Many people praise multitasking as a core career skill.

For the ADHD brain, multitasking is just rapid, high-energy attention drift.

Our brain lacks a robust background task scheduler.

When multiple tasks arrive at once, the prefrontal cortex tries to load them all into our tiny working memory. As our available RAM fills up, the brain enters panic mode, freezing our physical body while our mind spins in loops.

Stop calling yourself unproductive. We must manually suspend other tasks to enable **cognitive unloading**.

---

## 3 Steps to Clear Your Mental RAM

When your brain deadlocks from doing too much, follow this checklist:

### 1. Brain Dump: Suspend Background Processes
Don't let plans run in your head.
Open the [Brain Dump](https://adhdos.app/brain-dump) in ADHDOS and type out every "need to do" task currently flashing in your mind.
Writing them down signals to your brain: "These are saved; you can free the bandwidth they occupied in working memory."

### 2. Visual Filtering: Enter Zen Mode
Multitasking panic is fueled by visual clutter: other tabs, notifications, and desktop widgets.
Turn on **Zen Mode** in ADHDOS.
This hides every visual distraction except the current editor block. Eliminating visual noise drops the energy needed to resist distractions, forcing your brain into single-thread mode.

### 3. Single-Thread Lock: Set a Focus Clock
Pick the easiest task from your dumped list.
Set the [Focus Clock](https://adhdos.app/focus-clock) to 15 minutes.
Tell yourself: "For these 15 minutes, I don't care how urgent other things are. I will only do this tiny task. When it ends, I get a legal break."

Understand your brain's waves. Practice elastic focus, but lock one thread. Multi-threaded spinning is exhausting; single-threaded hacking gets it done.
