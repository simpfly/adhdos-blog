For the ADHD brain, online shopping is a perfect "dopamine vending machine."

We lack baseline dopamine, and "browsing - anticipating delivery - unboxing" provides constant, instant neural stimulation.

We aren't buying the "goods"; we are buying the multi-stage dopamine hit.

Standard financial columns advise "willpower" and "deprivation."

But this fails against ADHD biology. Forcing deprivation triggers worse rebound shopping later. We need **dopamine alignment** using low-friction redirections.

---

## Redirection Tricks for Impulsive Buying

Instead of resisting the urge to select, redirect the dopamine pipeline:

### 1. Build a 48-Hour Cart Freezer
Don't fight the urge to browse. Browsing releases dopamine.
But the rule is: **no immediate checkouts**.
Open the [Brain Dump](https://adhdos.app/brain-dump) in ADHDOS and write down the items and links you want.
This tells your brain, "These are saved; you can let them go from active memory." Let the cart cool down for 48 hours before deciding.

### 2. Energy Menu: Free Dopamine Substitutes
Shopping urges peak when you feel empty or bored.
Instead of resisting, go to your [Energy Menu](https://adhdos.app/energy-menu) and pick a free, fast alternative to stimulate your brain:
*   Do 5 jumping jacks.
*   Unbox an old delivery box sitting in the corner.
*   Play a favorite loud song.
*   Splash cold water on your face.
Use free dopamine to feed your brain's immediate sensory hunger.

### 3. Adopt "Good-Enoughism"
Accept that you sometimes need small purchases to stimulate your brain.
Drop the expectation of a perfect, zero-spending budget. Allow room for minor treats—that is "good-enoughism."
