Highly sensitive people (HSP) navigate the workplace with highly sensitive antennas.

We capture the slightest shift in office dynamics, managers' frustration, or coworkers' awkwardness.

This empathy drives us to "sacrifice ourselves to keep the peace," leading to chronic people-pleasing.

Yet, trying to make everyone happy at work is a guaranteed road to bankruptcy.

Our active working memory is occupied by over-analyzing others, dragging us into chronic social burnout.

We need **coping hacks** for active **cognitive unloading**.

---

## The HSP Guide to Workplace Boundaries

Don't force yourself to act tough. Build physical defense tools:

### 1. Separation of Tasks: Use the Reframer
At the heart of people-pleasing is taking responsibility for other people's emotions.
Use the [Cognitive Reframer](https://adhdos.app/reframer) in ADHDOS to separate your tasks:
*   **Anxiety Brain**: "If I refuse this task, they will think I am lazy or cold. They will hold a grudge."
*   **Reframed Reality**: "My schedule is saturated. Saying no is resource preservation. Their reaction is their task, not a measure of my worth."
*   **Anxiety Brain**: "My boss didn't say hi to me this morning. I must have messed up."
*   **Reframed Reality**: "The boss might be tired, under-rested, or stressed about their own KPIs. It has nothing to do with me."

### 2. The Board Shield: Copy-Paste Refusals
Often we fail to say no because we don't know how to phrase it politely.
Keep a few standard templates in your ADHDOS [Board](https://adhdos.app/board) and **copy-paste** them when tired:
*   *"Got it! This sounds like a great project. However, looking at my current commitments, I won't have the capacity to take this on this week."*
*   *"Understood! My plate is currently full. If we need to prioritize this task, let's look at which of my active projects we should push back."*
Using the Board as a shield reduces the executive energy needed to compose responses on the spot, preserving your battery.
