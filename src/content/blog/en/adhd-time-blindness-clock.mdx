For many with ADHD, time is not a continuous, flowing line.

We perceive time in only two states: **"Now"** and **"Not Now"**.

If an event is set for 3 PM, because it is "Not Now," we struggle to measure the space between 10 AM and 3 PM. This ambiguity triggers panic, locking us in a frozen "waiting mode."

Conversely, when hyperfocused, time becomes "Not Now" and vanishes entirely, leading to chronic lateness.

Trying to "force yourself to track time" only spikes anxiety. We need somatic **rhythm tracking** to build elastic time perception.

---

## Somatic Hacks to Tame Time Blindness

Since your internal clock is broken, make time visible:

### 1. The Focus Clock: Turn Time into Space
Do not rely on digital number clocks (like 14:15). Numbers are abstract symbols to ADHD brains.
Use the [Focus Clock](https://adhdos.app/focus-clock) in ADHDOS. It visualizes the passage of time via a shrinking progress bar.
Watching the bar physically shrink transmits a somatic sense of fading time, helping you disengage from hyperfocus smoothly.

### 2. Defeat Waiting Mode: Block Buffers in Your Calendar
Waiting mode is exhausting because your brain equates "waiting" with "constant vigilance."
Use the [Calendar](https://adhdos.app/calendar) in ADHDOS to set a physical buffer block (e.g., mark 1 hour before your meeting as a dedicated prep zone).
For the time before that buffer, tell yourself: "This is safe work/rest time; the calendar is watching, so I can stand down." This unloads the vigilance, permitting **elastic focus**.

### 3. Multiply Estimates by the "ADHD Coefficient"
When you estimate "getting ready takes 20 minutes," you only calculate transit time. Your brain forgets the friction of finding keys, putting on shoes, and waiting for elevators.
Multiply all your time estimates by 1.5 (the ADHD coefficient) to build a realistic timeline.
