In developmental biology, infants learn "object permanence" around age two—understanding that an object still exists even when hidden.

Yet for many with ADHD, we battle a lifelong "out of sight, out of mind" neuro-divergent reality in both physical and digital tasks.

Standard home organization preaches "hiding things away"—behind closed doors, inside opaque drawers, and on pristine clear desk surfaces.

For ADHD, this is a recipe for disaster. Once an item leaves our visual field, it drops out of our working memory. To prevent forgetting, we end up leaving everything on the counter, leading to chaotic piles.

Stop forcing yourself to adapt to neurotypical minimalism. Use **coping hacks** to enable visual **cognitive unloading**.

---

## Low-Friction Organizing Rules for Object Permanence

To align with the "out of sight, out of mind" trait, make your physical and mental space visible:

### 1. Physical Storage: Transparent & Lidless
*   **Dump opaque bins**: Replace them all with clear plastic bins or wire baskets. Your eyes must penetrate the container.
*   **Remove cabinet doors and lids**: Lids are the enemy of action. Adding a step to open a lid doubles the friction of taking or returning an item.

### 2. Digital Tasks: Visual Board Walls
If you bury your tasks inside folders or closed notebooks, they will gather dust.
Use the ADHDOS [Board](https://adhdos.app/board) module:
*   **Outsource your working memory**: Keep all raw ideas, immediate focus items, and templates pinned onto a visual board.
*   **Keep it visible**: Pin the Board to one side of your active work screen. Use a visible card wall to replace your fragile working memory, keeping tasks alive in your sight.

### 3. Pathway Placement
Put items you must remember (like pills, keys, or bills) directly on your physical pathway (e.g., right next to your mug, or hung on the door handle). Let the environment trigger your action.
